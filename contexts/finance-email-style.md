# Email Style Guide

## Tone
- Professional but approachable
- Concise — respect the reader's time
- Data-driven — reference specific numbers
- Action-oriented — always include next steps

## Structure
1. **Opening**: One sentence stating the purpose
2. **Key findings/data**: 2–3 bullet points with specifics
3. **Context**: Brief background if needed
4. **Action items**: Numbered list with owners/deadlines
5. **Close**: Offer to discuss further

## Rules
- Keep under 200 words unless complexity demands more
- Never bury the lead — important info goes first
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Bold key numbers and deadlines
- Avoid jargon unless writing to finance team
- Always proofread numbers — one wrong digit erodes trust
- CC sparingly — only people who need to act or know
- Reply-all only when everyone needs the update

## Common Templates

### Budget Update
Subject: [Month] Budget Update — [on track / X% over / X% under]

### Meeting Follow-up
Subject: Follow-up: [Meeting Topic] — Action Items

### Data Request Response
Subject: RE: [Topic] — Data Attached

### Escalation
Subject: [ATTENTION] [Topic] — Requires Decision by [Date]
