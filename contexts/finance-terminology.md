# Finance Terminology & Conventions

## Common Abbreviations
- **P&L** — Profit and Loss Statement
- **B/A** — Budget vs Actual
- **YTD** — Year to Date
- **MTD** — Month to Date
- **QTD** — Quarter to Date
- **FY** — Fiscal Year
- **CAPEX** — Capital Expenditure
- **OPEX** — Operating Expenditure
- **COGS** — Cost of Goods Sold
- **SG&A** — Selling, General & Administrative expenses
- **EBITDA** — Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization
- **WIP** — Work in Progress
- **AR** — Accounts Receivable
- **AP** — Accounts Payable
- **GL** — General Ledger
- **JE** — Journal Entry
- **TB** — Trial Balance

## Variance Analysis Rules
- Flag any line item with variance > 5% of budget
- For items > $100K, use absolute dollar thresholds too
- Always provide a root cause hypothesis for material variances
- Distinguish between timing variances and true variances

## Report Formatting
- Dollar amounts: use comma separators, 2 decimal places ($1,234.56)
- Percentages: 1 decimal place (12.3%)
- Negative numbers: parentheses, not minus sign — ($1,234) not -$1,234
- Headers: Bold, title case
- Tables: right-align numbers, left-align text

## Fiscal Calendar
- Fiscal year runs January–December
- Month-end close target: books closed by 5th business day
- Quarter-end: additional reconciliation and reporting
- Year-end: full audit prep, extended close period
